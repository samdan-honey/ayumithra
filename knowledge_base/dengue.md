# Dengue Fever

## What is Dengue?
Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection common in tropical and subtropical regions. It's spread by Aedes mosquitoes (the same mosquitoes that spread Zika and chikungunya). Dengue can range from mild to severe and potentially life-threatening.

## What Does Dengue Feel Like?
Symptoms appear 4-10 days after mosquito bite and last 2-7 days:

### Typical Symptoms:
- **Sudden high fever** (104°F or 40°C)
- **Severe headache** (especially behind the eyes)
- **Severe joint and muscle pain** (called "breakbone fever" because pain is so intense)
- **Pain behind the eyes**
- **Nausea and vomiting**
- **Swollen glands**
- **Rash** (appears 2-5 days after fever starts)
- **Extreme fatigue**

The pain in joints, muscles, and bones is what makes dengue特别uncomfortable.

## Warning Signs of Severe Dengue (Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever):
These usually appear 3-7 days after first symptoms, often as fever decreases:

### Go to Hospital IMMEDIATELY if you notice:
- **Severe abdominal pain**
- **Persistent vomiting** (3+ times in an hour or 4+ times in 6 hours)
- **Bleeding** from nose, gums, or in vomit/stool
- **Blood in urine**
- **Rapid breathing**
- **Cold, clammy skin**
- **Extreme restlessness** or irritability
- **Fatigue or weakness** that worsens
- **Feeling very thirsty** but unable to drink

Severe dengue is a medical emergency and can be fatal without proper treatment.

## How Do You Get Dengue?
- Bitten by an infected Aedes mosquito
- Mosquitoes bite during daytime (early morning and late afternoon)
- NOT spread directly from person to person
- Common in tropical areas: India, Southeast Asia, Latin America, Africa

## What to Do If You Suspect Dengue:

### See a Doctor:
- Get tested (blood test confirms dengue)
- Follow doctor's advice closely
- Monitor for warning signs

### Home Care for Mild Dengue:
- **Rest** - Don't exert yourself
- **Drink lots of fluids**: Water, ORS, coconut water, fruit juices
- **Paracetamol** for fever and pain
- **Monitor temperature** regularly
- **Prevent mosquito bites** (so you don't spread it to others)

### What NOT to Take:
- **NO aspirin** - increases bleeding risk
- **NO ibuprofen** or NSAIDs - can worsen bleeding
- Only use paracetamol/acetaminophen for fever

## How to Manage Fever:
- Take paracetamol as directed (don't exceed recommended dose)
- Use lukewarm cloth to wipe body if fever is very high
- Wear light clothing
- Keep room cool and well-ventilated

## Hydration is Critical:
Drink at least 8-10 glasses of fluid daily:
- Oral rehydration solution (ORS)
- Coconut water
- Fresh fruit juices
- Clear soups
- Water with lemon and salt

## How Long Does It Last?
- **Acute phase** (fever): 2-7 days
- **Recovery phase**: 1-2 weeks
- **Fatigue** may last several weeks after recovery

## Prevention:

### Avoid Mosquito Bites:
- Use mosquito repellent (DEET, picaridin, or oil of lemon eucalyptus)
- Wear long-sleeved shirts and long pants
- Use mosquito nets while sleeping
- Install window and door screens
- Use air conditioning if available

### Eliminate Breeding Sites:
- Remove standing water (mosquitoes breed in stagnant water)
- Empty flower pots, buckets, tires regularly
- Cover water storage containers
- Clean pet water bowls frequently
- Check areas around your home weekly

### Community Efforts:
- Support fogging and mosquito control programs
- Keep neighborhood clean
- Report dengue cases to health authorities

## No Vaccine Widely Available:
While some dengue vaccines exist, they're not widely available or recommended for everyone. Prevention through mosquito bite avoidance is still the best approach.

## Can You Get Dengue More Than Once?
Yes, there are 4 different dengue virus serotypes. Getting one type gives you immunity to that type only, but increases risk of severe dengue if you get infected with a different type later.

## Recovery and Aftercare:
- Rest for at least 2 weeks after fever subsides
- Continue drinking plenty of fluids
- Gradually resume normal activities
- Follow up with doctor for blood tests
- Fatigue and weakness may persist for weeks

## When Can You Return to Work/School?
- After fever is gone for at least 24 hours (without medication)
- When you feel strong enough
- Continue preventing mosquito bites for 2 weeks

## Important:
Dengue can progress rapidly. Even if you feel okay, monitor yourself closely for the first week. If any warning signs appear, go to hospital immediately - don't wait.
