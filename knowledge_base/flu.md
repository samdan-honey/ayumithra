# Flu (Influenza)

## What is the Flu?
Influenza, commonly called the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. It's more serious than a common cold and can make you feel very sick. The flu comes on suddenly and can last 1-2 weeks.

## What Does the Flu Feel Like?
Unlike a cold that develops slowly, flu symptoms hit you quickly. You might feel fine in the morning and terrible by afternoon. Common symptoms include:

- **Sudden high fever** (100-102°F or higher)
- **Chills and shivering** (feeling very cold)
- **Severe body aches** (especially in back, arms, and legs)
- **Headache** (often severe)
- **Extreme tiredness** and weakness
- **Dry, persistent cough**
- **Sore throat**
- **Runny or stuffy nose**
- **Loss of appetite**
- **Sometimes nausea or vomiting** (more common in children)

The key difference from a cold: flu makes you feel exhausted and achy all over, not just sniffly.

## How is It Different from a Cold?
- **Flu**: Sudden onset, high fever, severe body aches, extreme fatigue
- **Cold**: Gradual onset, no or low fever, mild aches, able to function normally

## How Do You Catch the Flu?
The flu spreads when:
- An infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks near you
- You touch surfaces with the virus, then touch your mouth, nose, or eyes
- You're in crowded places during flu season (winter months)

You're most contagious in the first 3-4 days after getting sick.

## How to Feel Better:

### Rest and Recovery:
- **Stay in bed**: Your body needs all its energy to fight the virus
- **Don't go to work or school**: Rest and prevent spreading it
- **Sleep as much as possible**

### Stay Hydrated:
- Drink plenty of water
- Warm soups and broths
- Herbal teas
- Oral rehydration solutions if you have fever

### Manage Symptoms:
- **For fever and body aches**: Paracetamol or ibuprofen
- **For cough**: Honey, warm fluids, cough suppressants
- **For congestion**: Steam inhalation, decongestants
- **For sore throat**: Salt water gargles, throat lozenges

### What Helps:
- Keep room warm but well-ventilated
- Use a humidifier if air is dry
- Eat light, nutritious foods when you can
- Take warm baths to ease body aches

## What NOT to Do:
- Don't take antibiotics (they don't work on viruses)
- Don't push yourself to work or exercise
- Don't ignore severe symptoms
- Don't give aspirin to children or teenagers

## How Long Does It Last?
- **Days 1-3**: Sudden fever, chills, body aches, headache
- **Days 3-5**: Fever decreases, respiratory symptoms increase
- **Days 5-7**: Cough and fatigue continue
- **Week 2**: Gradual recovery, tiredness may linger

Most people recover in 1-2 weeks, but fatigue can last longer.

## Prevention:

### Get Vaccinated:
- Annual flu vaccine is the best protection
- Reduces severity even if you get the flu
- Recommended for everyone over 6 months

### Daily Prevention:
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid touching your face
- Stay away from sick people
- Don't share personal items
- Clean frequently touched surfaces
- Maintain good health with sleep, exercise, and nutrition

## When to See a Doctor Immediately:

### For Adults:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Chest pain or pressure
- Confusion or dizziness
- Severe vomiting
- Symptoms improve but then return with worse fever and cough
- Fever above 103°F (39.4°C)

### For Children:
- Fast breathing or trouble breathing
- Bluish skin color
- Not drinking enough fluids
- Not waking up or not interacting
- Fever with rash
- Symptoms improve but then return worse

## High-Risk Groups:
Some people are more likely to have serious flu complications:
- Pregnant women
- Children under 5 (especially under 2)
- Adults over 65
- People with asthma, diabetes, heart disease
- People with weakened immune systems

If you're in a high-risk group, see a doctor within 48 hours of symptoms starting.

## Can You Get the Flu More Than Once?
Yes, because flu viruses change every year. That's why you need a flu vaccine annually.

## Is It Dangerous?
For healthy adults, the flu is usually not life-threatening but makes you very sick. However, it can lead to serious complications like pneumonia, especially in high-risk groups. Take it seriously and rest properly.
