# Migraine

## What is a Migraine?
A migraine is not just a "bad headache" - it's a complex neurological condition that causes severe, debilitating pain. Migraines often affect one side of the head and can last from 4 hours to 3 days. They come with other symptoms that make them much worse than regular headaches.

## What Does a Migraine Feel Like?

### The Headache:
- **Throbbing or pulsing pain** (like something is pounding in your head)
- **Usually on one side** of the head (left or right)
- **Moderate to severe intensity** - makes it hard to do normal activities
- **Gets worse with movement**, light, or noise

### Other Symptoms:
- **Nausea** (feeling sick to your stomach)
- **Vomiting** (throwing up)
- **Extreme sensitivity to light** - bright lights hurt your eyes
- **Extreme sensitivity to sound** - normal noises feel too loud
- **Sensitivity to smells** - odors can make it worse
- **Blurred vision**
- **Dizziness** or feeling lightheaded
- **Aura** (some people): Seeing flashing lights, zigzag patterns, or experiencing temporary vision loss before the headache starts

### Before the Migraine (Warning Signs):
Some people notice signs hours or days before:
- Mood changes (irritability, depression, or euphoria)
- Food cravings
- Neck stiffness
- Frequent yawning
- Increased thirst and urination

## What Triggers Migraines?
Common triggers include:
- **Stress** or anxiety
- **Lack of sleep** or too much sleep
- **Hormonal changes** in women (menstruation, pregnancy)
- **Certain foods**: Aged cheese, processed meats, chocolate, alcohol (especially red wine)
- **Skipping meals** or fasting
- **Dehydration**
- **Strong smells** (perfume, smoke, chemicals)
- **Bright or flashing lights**
- **Weather changes**
- **Caffeine** (too much or withdrawal)

## What Helps During a Migraine:

### Immediate Relief:
- **Rest in a dark, quiet room** - this is the most important thing
- **Sleep** if you can - many migraines end after sleep
- **Cold compress** on your forehead or neck
- **Drink water** - dehydration makes it worse
- **Caffeine** (small amount) can help in early stages

### Medications:
- **Over-the-counter**: Ibuprofen, paracetamol, or aspirin (take at first sign)
- **Prescription**: Triptans (specific migraine medication)
- **Anti-nausea medicine** if you're vomiting

### Natural Remedies:
- Ginger tea for nausea
- Peppermint oil on temples (some people find relief)
- Magnesium supplements (prevention)
- Regular sleep schedule

## What NOT to Do:
- Don't ignore early warning signs
- Don't take painkillers more than 2-3 days per week (can cause rebound headaches)
- Don't drink alcohol during a migraine
- Don't stare at screens (phone, computer, TV)
- Don't skip meals

## How Long Do Migraines Last?
- **Without treatment**: 4 to 72 hours
- **With early treatment**: Can be reduced to 1-2 hours
- **Recovery phase**: After pain stops, you may feel drained for a day

## How to Prevent Migraines:

### Lifestyle Changes:
- **Regular sleep**: Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily
- **Don't skip meals**: Eat at regular intervals
- **Stay hydrated**: Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily
- **Manage stress**: Yoga, meditation, deep breathing
- **Regular exercise**: But don't overexert
- **Keep a migraine diary**: Track what triggers your migraines

### Foods to Avoid (if they trigger you):
- Aged cheeses
- Processed meats (hot dogs, salami)
- Foods with MSG
- Artificial sweeteners
- Red wine and beer
- Excessive caffeine

### Preventive Medications:
If you get migraines more than 4 times a month, doctors may prescribe:
- Blood pressure medications
- Antidepressants
- Anti-seizure medications
- CGRP inhibitors (new migraine-specific drugs)

## When to See a Doctor:

### See a doctor if:
- You get migraines more than once a week
- Over-the-counter medicines don't help
- Migraines interfere with your daily life
- You need painkillers more than 2 days per week

### Go to Emergency if:
- **"Worst headache of your life"** (sudden, severe)
- Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion, or seizures
- Headache after head injury
- Weakness, numbness, or trouble speaking
- Headache that gets progressively worse over days

## Migraines vs Regular Headache:
- **Migraine**: One-sided, throbbing, with nausea, light/sound sensitivity, lasts hours to days
- **Tension headache**: Both sides, pressure/tightness, no nausea, mild to moderate

## Are Migraines Dangerous?
Migraines themselves are not life-threatening, but they significantly reduce quality of life. However, if your headache pattern changes suddenly or you experience unusual symptoms, get medical attention to rule out other causes.

## Can Children Get Migraines?
Yes, children can get migraines too. In children, the pain may be on both sides and last less time. Children may also have abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting without headache (abdominal migraine).

## Is There a Cure?
There's no cure for migraines, but they can be managed effectively with the right combination of lifestyle changes, trigger avoidance, and medications. Many people find that migraines decrease as they age.
