# Food Poisoning

## What is Food Poisoning?
Food poisoning happens when you eat or drink something contaminated with harmful bacteria, viruses, or parasites. It makes your stomach and intestines sick. Food poisoning can start within hours of eating contaminated food and usually lasts 1-3 days.

## What Does Food Poisoning Feel Like?
Symptoms usually start 1-6 hours after eating bad food (sometimes up to 48 hours):

- **Nausea** (feeling like you need to vomit)
- **Vomiting** (throwing up)
- **Diarrhea** (watery, loose stools)
- **Stomach cramps and pain** (can be severe)
- **Loss of appetite**
- **Fever** (sometimes)
- **Weakness and fatigue**
- **Headache**

The symptoms can range from mild to severe depending on what contaminated your food.

## Common Causes:

### Bacteria:
- **Salmonella**: Raw eggs, undercooked poultry, unpasteurized milk
- **E. coli**: Undercooked beef, raw vegetables, contaminated water
- **Listeria**: Deli meats, soft cheeses, hot dogs
- **Campylobacter**: Raw or undercooked poultry

### Viruses:
- **Norovirus**: Very contagious, spreads easily
- **Hepatitis A**: Contaminated water or food

### Common Sources:
- Undercooked meat, poultry, or eggs
- Raw or undercooked seafood
- Unpasteurized (raw) milk
- Raw sprouts
- unwashed fruits and vegetables
- Food left out too long at room temperature
- Contaminated water

## What to Do:

### First 24 Hours:
- **Stop eating** for a few hours to let your stomach settle
- **Sip clear fluids**: Water, oral rehydration solution (ORS), clear broth
- **Don't force yourself to eat** - focus on staying hydrated
- **Rest** - your body is fighting infection

### When You Can Eat:
Start with bland, easy-to-digest foods:
- Bananas
- Rice (white)
- Applesauce
- Toast (plain)
- Crackers
- Boiled potatoes

### What to Drink:
- Oral rehydration solution (best option)
- Water (sip slowly)
- Clear broths
- Weak tea without caffeine
- Coconut water

**Important**: Sip slowly - don't gulp large amounts at once

## What NOT to Do:
- Don't take anti-diarrhea medicine immediately (your body is trying to flush out toxins)
- Don't eat dairy products initially
- Don't eat spicy, fatty, or fried foods
- Don't drink alcohol or caffeine
- Don't eat until vomiting stops

## How Long Does It Last?
- **Mild cases**: 12-48 hours
- **Moderate cases**: 1-3 days
- **Severe cases**: Up to a week
- Most people start feeling better within 24-48 hours

## Home Remedies That Help:
- **Ginger tea**: Reduces nausea
- **Lemon water**: Has antibacterial properties
- **Cumin water**: Aids digestion
- **Bananas**: Replace lost potassium
- **Honey**: Has antibacterial properties
- **Apple cider vinegar**: Mix 2 tablespoons in warm water

## When to See a Doctor Immediately:

### Go to hospital if you have:
- **Blood in vomit or stool**
- **High fever** (above 101.5°F or 38.6°C)
- **Diarrhea lasting more than 3 days**
- **Signs of dehydration**:
  - Very dry mouth and throat
  - Not urinating or very dark urine
  - Dizziness when standing
  - Confusion
- **Blurred vision**
- **Muscle weakness**
- **Severe stomach pain** that doesn't improve

### High-Risk People - See Doctor Early:
- Babies and young children
- Pregnant women
- Adults over 65
- People with weak immune systems
- People with chronic diseases (diabetes, kidney disease)

## How to Prevent Food Poisoning:

### Food Safety:
- **Wash hands** before handling food
- **Cook food thoroughly**:
  - Meat to proper temperature
  - Eggs until firm
- **Separate raw and cooked foods**: Use different cutting boards
- **Refrigerate promptly**: Don't leave food out more than 2 hours
- **Wash fruits and vegetables** before eating
- **Check expiration dates**
- **Don't eat food with unusual smell, color, or texture**

### When Eating Out:
- Choose clean, reputable restaurants
- Avoid street food in areas with poor hygiene
- Make sure food is served hot
- Avoid raw or undercooked items

### In the Kitchen:
- Keep kitchen surfaces clean
- Use separate cutting boards for meat and vegetables
- Wash dish towels frequently
- Defrost food in refrigerator, not on counter
- When in doubt, throw it out

## Is It Contagious?
Some types of food poisoning (like norovirus) are very contagious. Others (like salmonella from undercooked chicken) are not spread from person to person. Always wash hands thoroughly if you're sick.

## Can You Get It Again?
Yes, there are many different bacteria and viruses that cause food poisoning. Having it once doesn't protect you from getting it again.

## Long-Term Effects:
Most people recover completely. However, severe cases can lead to:
- Dehydration requiring hospitalization
- Kidney problems (rare)
- Arthritis (in some salmonella cases)
- Neurological issues (very rare)

## Important Note:
If multiple people who ate the same food get sick, report it to local health authorities. This could indicate a larger food safety issue.
