# COVID-19

## What is COVID-19?
COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It can range from mild (like a cold) to severe (requiring hospitalization). The virus spreads easily from person to person through respiratory droplets.

## Common Symptoms:

### Most Common:
- **Fever** (temperature above 100.4°F or 38°C)
- **Dry cough** (persistent, doesn't produce mucus)
- **Fatigue** (extreme tiredness)
- **Body aches** and muscle pain
- **Headache**
- **Sore throat**
- **Loss of taste or smell** (very characteristic of COVID)

### Less Common:
- **Shortness of breath** or difficulty breathing
- **Congestion** or runny nose
- **Nausea** or vomiting
- **Diarrhea**
- **Chills**
- **Repeated shaking with chills**
- **Loss of appetite**

### Emergency Warning Signs - Seek Immediate Care:
- Trouble breathing
- Persistent pain or pressure in chest
- New confusion
- Inability to wake or stay awake
- Bluish lips or face

## How It Spreads:
- Breathing in air when close to an infected person
- Droplets from coughing, sneezing, talking
- Touching contaminated surfaces, then touching face
- Can spread even before symptoms appear
- Most contagious in first 5-7 days of illness

## What to Do If You Think You Have COVID:

### Get Tested:
- Take a rapid antigen test or PCR test
- Isolate while waiting for results

### If Positive - Isolate:
- Stay home for at least 5 days from symptom onset
- Stay in a separate room if living with others
- Use a separate bathroom if possible
- Wear a mask if you must be around others
- Don't share personal items

### Mild Cases - Home Care:
- **Rest** - Get plenty of sleep
- **Hydrate** - Drink lots of fluids
- **Monitor symptoms** - Check temperature twice daily
- **Medications**: Paracetamol for fever and pain
- **Isolate** - Don't go to work, school, or public areas

### Help Breathing:
- Sit upright, don't lie flat
- Practice deep breathing exercises
- If you have a pulse oximeter, check oxygen levels (should be above 94%)

## When to Seek Medical Care:

### Contact Doctor If:
- Symptoms don't improve after 5 days
- Fever persists more than 3 days
- You're in a high-risk group

### Go to Hospital Immediately If:
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Oxygen saturation below 94%
- Chest pain or pressure
- Confusion or disorientation
- Bluish lips or face
- Unable to stay awake

## High-Risk Groups (More Likely to Get Severe):
- Adults 65 and older
- People with underlying conditions:
  - Heart disease
  - Diabetes
  - Obesity
  - Chronic lung disease
  - Cancer
  - Weakened immune system
- Pregnant women
- Smokers

## How Long Does It Last?
- **Mild cases**: 1-2 weeks
- **Moderate cases**: 2-4 weeks
- **Severe cases**: Weeks to months
- **Recovery varies** - some feel better quickly, others take longer

## Long COVID:
Some people experience symptoms for weeks or months:
- Persistent fatigue
- Brain fog (difficulty concentrating)
- Shortness of breath
- Joint pain
- Sleep problems

## Prevention:

### Vaccination:
- Get vaccinated and boosted when eligible
- Reduces severity even if you get infected

### Daily Precautions:
- Wear masks in crowded indoor spaces
- Wash hands frequently (20 seconds with soap)
- Maintain good ventilation indoors
- Stay home when sick
- Get tested if exposed or symptomatic

### Boost Immunity:
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
- Balanced nutrition
- Regular exercise
- Manage stress
- Stay hydrated

## Treatment Options:

### For Mild Cases:
- Rest and fluids
- Paracetamol for fever/pain
- Cough medicine if needed
- Monitor symptoms

### For High-Risk Patients:
- Antiviral medications (within 5 days of symptoms)
- Monoclonal antibody therapy
- Requires doctor's prescription

### For Severe Cases (Hospital):
- Oxygen therapy
- Steroids
- Blood thinners
- Ventilator support if needed

## When Can You End Isolation?
- At least 5 days since symptoms started
- Fever-free for 24 hours (without medication)
- Other symptoms improving
- Wear mask around others through day 10

## Can You Get COVID More Than Once?
Yes, reinfections are possible, especially with new variants. Vaccination and previous infection provide some protection but not complete immunity.

## Important Notes:
- COVID symptoms are similar to flu and cold - testing is the only way to confirm
- You can spread it even without symptoms
- Variants may cause different symptom patterns
- Follow local health authority guidelines

## Mental Health:
Isolation and illness can affect mental health:
- Stay connected with family/friends virtually
- Maintain routines
- Practice relaxation techniques
- Seek help if feeling overwhelmed or depressed
