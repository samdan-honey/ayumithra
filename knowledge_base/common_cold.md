# Common Cold

## What is the Common Cold?
The common cold is a mild viral infection that affects your nose, throat, and sinuses. It's the most common illness people get, and while it's uncomfortable, it usually goes away on its own within 7-10 days.

## What Does It Feel Like?
When you have a cold, you might experience:
- Runny or stuffy nose (your nose might drip or feel blocked)
- Sore or scratchy throat
- Sneezing frequently
- Mild cough that gets worse at night
- Watery eyes
- Slight headache
- Feeling tired or weak
- Low-grade fever (sometimes)

The symptoms usually start 1-3 days after you catch the virus.

## What Causes It?
Colds are caused by viruses, not by being cold or wet. You can catch a cold when:
- Someone sick sneezes or coughs near you
- You touch something with the virus on it, then touch your face
- You're in close contact with someone who has a cold

## How Can You Feel Better?

### At Home:
- **Rest**: Your body needs energy to fight the virus. Sleep as much as you need.
- **Drink fluids**: Water, warm tea, soup, or juice help thin mucus and prevent dehydration
- **Gargle salt water**: Mix 1/2 teaspoon salt in warm water. Gargle to soothe sore throat
- **Use honey**: One spoon of honey can calm cough (not for children under 1 year)
- **Steam inhalation**: Breathe in steam from hot water to clear blocked nose
- **Warm fluids**: Hot tea, soup, or warm water with lemon feel soothing

### Over-the-Counter Help:
- Pain relievers like paracetamol for headache or fever
- Decongestants for blocked nose
- Cough syrup if cough is bothering you

## What NOT to Do:
- Don't take antibiotics - they don't work on viruses
- Don't smoke or be around smoke
- Don't ignore worsening symptoms

## How to Prevent Colds:
- Wash your hands frequently with soap
- Don't touch your eyes, nose, or mouth with dirty hands
- Avoid close contact with sick people
- Don't share towels, cups, or utensils
- Keep your immunity strong with good sleep and healthy food

## When Should You See a Doctor?
Visit a doctor if:
- Your symptoms last more than 10 days without improvement
- You have a high fever (above 101.3°F or 38.5°C)
- Your symptoms get worse after a few days
- You have difficulty breathing or wheezing
- You have severe headache, sinus pain, or ear pain
- You're coughing up thick, colored mucus for many days

## Is It Serious?
For most people, the common cold is not serious. It's annoying and uncomfortable, but your body will fight it off. However, babies, elderly people, and those with weak immune systems need to be more careful.

## Can You Go to Work or School?
If you have a fever, stay home. If you just have a runny nose and mild cough, you can go but:
- Wash hands frequently
- Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing
- Avoid close contact with others

## How to Stop It From Spreading:
- Cover your mouth and nose when sneezing or coughing
- Use tissues and throw them away immediately
- Wash hands often
- Stay home if you have a fever
- Don't share personal items
